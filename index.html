When is pride month?

In 2000, President Bill Clinton delcared the summer month of June as Gay and Lesbian Pride Month.

Pride month consists of parties, workshops, conerts, and parades. Every state celebrates pride differents days in the month of June.

Why is pride month in June?

Pride month was inspired by the Sontewall Uprising. The purpose of the riots and pride month is to notice the impact of LGBTQ community on society. Pride month symbolized political protest against unjust systems, while being fun and cheerful.

Stonewall Uprising

It all started with the Stonewall Uprising in the 1960s. The Stonewall Inn, in Greenwich Village, was a gay club for the local LGBTQ community. On June 28, 1969 the inn was raided by the New York police causing anger and fury among the LGBTQ community starting the Stonewall Riots.

New Orleans Pride History

In February of 1971 the Gay Liberation Front of New Orleans hosted a gay-in picnic in the beautiful City Park. This event and many others created the Southern Decadence tradition.

Southern Decadence

On a hot Labor Day weekend in 1972 a group of Tulane students threw a themed party where everyone had to dress as your favorite Southern Decadent. This gathering was so successful they started having a parade through the French Quarter. In 1974 Souther Decadence was officially a part of New Orleans Pride.
